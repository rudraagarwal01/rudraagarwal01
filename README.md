### 💡 About Me

I'm a Computer Science student at the University of Maryland studying Machine Learning with minors in Business and Information Risk Management, Ethics, and Privacy. I'm drawn to the tech industry for a simple reason: building things has always felt like the most direct way to turn curiosity into impact. 

My goal is to use technological advancements to solve real-world problems. I gravitate toward problems where the "right" answer isn't obvious, where you have to test, learn, and iterate until something actually works for real people. A big part of my identity is showing up consistently. Progress is usually made in the unglamorous moments: debugging a driver issue, tracing a network configuration, or cleaning up an integration. I'm not chasing perfect code on the first try; I'm chasing clarity, reliability, and a system I can explain and maintain. Through my work and projects, I've seen that technology is only "smart" if it's usable, inclusive, and built with empathy.
